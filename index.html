About Me

Hi, my name is Chad Kaplan. I am an aspiring coder. I grew up in Coral Springs, Florida and have lived in the sunshine state my entire life. I grew up playing basketball, volleyball, playing a lot of video games, and petting every dog I can get my hands on. After graduating high school, I moved to Orlando for college at UCF in the Fall of 2011, where I earned a bachelor’s degree in communication sciences and disorders. When I continued on to graduate school in pursuit of my master’s degree to become a speech-language pathologist, I started to discover more about myself and the aspects of a career that I find appealing or repelling. I ended up leaving the program in pursuit of a more fulfilling field for me.
It wasn’t until recently that I discovered how my interests coincide with coding and how I could see myself writing lines of code for a living and never calling it “work”. I have always loved technology but have never been extremely knowledgeable on the subject outside of the norm of “millennial tech savvy skills”. I have always been able to bet on my intelligence to be able to learn any skill or subject I want, however; I have not always been good at knowing what I should learn. Throughout the UCF Coding Bootcamp I hope to acquire the skills to become a competent coder in order to get a job that I enjoy doing every day.
